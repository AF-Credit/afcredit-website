Agricultural Tie Mortgage:
Can You Get One?

Properties with an agricultural occupancy condition often sell at discounts of 20–40% to equivalent unrestricted homes. Mortgage options are more limited — but a mainstream decline does not mean the purchase cannot proceed.

At AF Credit, we specialise in helping buyers purchase properties that mainstream lenders struggle to finance — including agricultural tie properties, rural homes, planning-restricted assets, auction purchases and other mortgage-restricted assets.

Quick answer: Can you get a mortgage on a property with an agricultural tie?

Yes, but mortgage options are often more limited. Many lenders view agricultural tie properties as higher risk because occupancy restrictions reduce the pool of eligible future buyers. Fewer lenders may be available, deposits may need to be larger, and specialist underwriting is often required. Where mainstream lenders decline, bridging finance can often provide a practical route to purchase.

What Is an Agricultural Tie?

An agricultural tie — formally known as an Agricultural Occupancy Condition (AOC) — is a planning restriction attached to a property that limits who can legally occupy it. The condition typically requires the property to be occupied by someone employed in agriculture, forestry or another qualifying rural occupation, or by a dependant of such a person. Agricultural ties commonly arise on farmhouses, smallholdings, rural cottages, former farm workers' dwellings, equestrian properties and agricultural development sites.

The exact wording varies between conditions and should always be reviewed by a solicitor before purchase — the restriction may be narrower or broader than it first appears.

Why Do Agricultural Ties Affect Mortgages?

Lenders use the property as security for their loan. Their primary concern with agricultural tie properties is marketability. If the lender ever needed to repossess and sell the property, the occupancy restriction would significantly reduce the number of eligible buyers — limiting the lender's ability to recover their loan. This reduced effective security value leads many lenders to apply additional restrictions or decline the application entirely.

Why Are Agricultural Tie Properties Cheaper?

Agricultural occupancy conditions typically reduce market values by between 20% and 40% compared to equivalent unrestricted homes, although the exact discount depends on location, demand and the specific wording of the restriction. The discount exists for the same reason lenders are cautious: fewer people can legally occupy the property, which reduces buyer competition and mortgage availability.

For informed buyers who qualify under the occupancy condition — or who are pursuing removal of the tie — this discount represents a significant opportunity.

Routes to Removing an Agricultural Tie

Certificate of Lawfulness

Where the occupancy condition has been continuously breached for the required period (typically 10 years), an application can be made for a Certificate of Lawfulness — confirming the breach has become lawful and the condition is no longer enforceable.

Planning Application

An application may be submitted to the local planning authority to remove or vary the agricultural occupancy condition. Success typically requires demonstrating that the condition is no longer necessary or that it unduly restricts the property's use.

Agricultural Need Assessment

Evidence demonstrating there is no longer any realistic agricultural demand for the property in the local area can support a removal application — particularly where the land has been separated from the property and agricultural activity has ceased.

No guarantee of removal

Agricultural tie removal is not guaranteed and is a matter for the local planning authority. Specialist planning advice should always be obtained before relying on removal as an exit strategy. Bridging finance with removal as the primary exit should be approached cautiously — a secondary exit (such as sale to a qualifying occupier) should also be considered.

What Happens When a Lender Identifies an Agricultural Tie?

Most issues arise during the mortgage valuation. The valuer identifies the agricultural occupancy condition in the planning register, notes it in their report, and the lender reviews its lending policy against the restriction. The lender may request additional information, refer the case for specialist underwriting, reduce the valuation to reflect the restriction, restrict lending or decline the application outright. This often surprises buyers who were unaware of the planning condition when they first viewed the property.

Agricultural Tie Properties at Auction

Agricultural tie properties appear regularly at auction because conventional mortgage finance is difficult to arrange. Auction purchases require completion within 28 days, which makes bridging finance the most practical funding solution for time-critical acquisitions. Many experienced rural investors specifically target agricultural tie properties at auction, where reduced competition can deliver the most attractive pricing relative to unrestricted value.

Why Investors Buy Agricultural Tie Properties

The planning restriction that restricts the mortgage also creates the pricing opportunity. Experienced rural investors understand that agricultural occupancy conditions can sometimes be removed, that the discount often substantially exceeds the cost of the planning process, and that the underlying property — once unrestricted — has genuine open market value. For investors willing to accept the planning risk and access specialist finance, agricultural tie properties can offer some of the most attractive risk-adjusted returns available in the UK residential market.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you get a mortgage on a property with an agricultural tie?

Yes, although lender choice is more limited than for standard residential property. Some lenders will lend where the buyer qualifies under the occupancy condition. See our unmortgageable property guide for a broader overview of planning-restricted assets.

What is an agricultural occupancy condition?

A planning restriction limiting occupation to people employed in agriculture, forestry or other qualifying rural occupations — or their dependants. The exact wording varies and should always be reviewed by a solicitor before purchase.

Why was my mortgage declined because of an agricultural tie?

Lenders are concerned about marketability. The occupancy restriction reduces the pool of eligible buyers and, in a repossession scenario, limits the lender's ability to sell at open market value. This makes the property a higher-risk security.

Do agricultural ties reduce property value?

Yes. Discounts of 20–40% to unrestricted equivalent value are typical, although the exact discount varies by location and restriction wording. The discount is precisely what creates the opportunity for buyers who can access specialist finance.

Can an agricultural tie be removed?

Sometimes — through a Certificate of Lawfulness, a planning application or by demonstrating there is no longer any agricultural demand. Specialist planning advice is essential. See our building regulations guide for more on how planning and compliance issues affect mortgageability more broadly.
