How Long Does a Bridging Loan Take?

Most bridging loans complete in 7–14 working days. The fastest complete in 5 working days. The slowest — complex cases with title issues or full RICS valuations — take 3–4 weeks. Here is what drives the timeline and how to move faster.

The short answer

Straightforward bridging loans on standard residential property typically complete in 7–14 working days. The fastest complete in 5 working days using AVM valuation and no-search indemnity insurance. Complex cases — semi-commercial, adverse title, multiple charges — may take 3–4 weeks.

The timeline is driven by three things: the valuation method, the legal process, and the speed at which all parties respond. Each can be compressed or stretched significantly.

The three stages of a bridging loan

Stage 1: Credit assessment and offer (1–3 days)

From the moment you make an enquiry to the point you receive a formal credit offer, most direct bridging lenders take 1–3 working days. AF Credit issues same-day indicative terms following an initial call or email, and formal credit offers within 24–48 hours of receiving the valuation and supporting documents.

Delays at this stage are usually caused by incomplete information — missing property details, unclear exit strategy, or a borrower who has not yet instructed solicitors.

Stage 2: Valuation (0–10 days)

Stage 3: Legal completion (3–15 days)

The legal process involves title review, search results (or no-search indemnity), pre-completion conditions, and the completion statement. In straightforward cases with responsive solicitors, this takes 3–5 working days. Two factors can extend it significantly:

  • Local authority searches — required by lenders unless replaced by no-search indemnity insurance. Some local authorities return searches in 2 days; others take 3–4 weeks. If speed matters, a no-search indemnity (typically £100–250) removes this wait entirely.
  • Title issues — missing title documents, adverse entries, lease extension requirements or restrictive covenants all require resolution. Clean freehold or long leasehold title is the fastest to work through.
The fastest possible bridging loan: 5 working days

AVM valuation (same day) + no-search indemnity (same day) + clean title + responsive solicitors + direct lender with delegated authority. AF Credit has completed on this basis in 5 working days on standard residential property at 60% LTV.

What typically slows things down

Waiting for a full RICS valuation. If you need 75% LTV, a physical inspection is non-negotiable. Surveyor availability varies — in some postcodes and at certain times of year, appointments are available within 2 days; in others, the wait is over a week.

Local authority search delays. Some councils are chronically slow. If speed is critical, instruct your solicitor to use a no-search indemnity from day one rather than waiting to see how long searches take.

Incomplete documents from the borrower. Every day a lender or solicitor spends waiting for a missing document is a day added to the timeline. Have your ID, proof of address, property details and exit strategy documented before you make the first call.

Slow solicitors. Bridging lenders with fast in-house processes are regularly slowed by conveyancers who are not experienced in bridging timelines. Instruct a solicitor with bridging experience who understands that 5-day deadlines are real.

Committee credit approval. Some lenders cannot issue a credit decision without a formal credit committee review — which may only convene weekly. Direct lenders with delegated authority can approve the same day.

How to make your bridging loan faster

  • Use AVM or desktop valuation where your LTV allows it
  • Instruct a no-search indemnity from the outset rather than waiting for searches
  • Instruct solicitors on the day you accept indicative terms
  • Provide all documents — ID, address, property details, exit evidence — at the first request, not piecemeal
  • Use a direct lender that can issue credit decisions the same day
  • Make contact before the deadline, not on the day
