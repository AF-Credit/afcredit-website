The Problem With Small Bridging Loans and Fees

Bridging finance has a fee problem that very few guides talk about openly. Most of the costs involved — arrangement fees, valuation fees, legal fees — are either fixed or only marginally variable. As a result, on a small loan they represent a disproportionately large percentage of what you're actually borrowing.

Consider the numbers:

The valuation fee alone can add 3% or more to the effective cost of a small bridging loan — before you've paid a penny in interest or arrangement fees. This is why fee structure matters far more than headline rate on small bridging loans. A lender offering 0.79%/month with a £0 valuation fee will often be significantly cheaper overall than one offering 0.69%/month with a £1,500 valuation fee and a 1–2% arrangement fee on top.

Common Uses for Small Bridging Loans

The use cases for a small bridging loan are the same as for larger amounts — the loan simply reflects a lower property value or higher equity position:

  • Auction purchases — buying a low-value lot at auction and needing to complete within 28 days. Our auction finance guide covers this in full.
  • Chain breaks — purchasing a new property before an existing one has sold, where the loan needed is small because of significant equity already held
  • Short-lease flats — buying a flat with a short lease that is unmortgageable, intending to extend the lease and then refinance onto a standard mortgage
  • Unmortgageable property — purchasing a property a mainstream lender will not touch, with the intention of bringing it to mortgageable standard. See our unmortgageable property guide.
  • Light refurbishment — funding a quick refurbishment to bring a property to lettable or saleable condition before refinancing. See our refurbishment bridging loans page.
  • Probate and estate sales — purchasing a property from an estate quickly, where competitive pricing reflects the seller's need for speed

Which Valuation Route Makes Sense?

On a small loan, the valuation route decision is particularly important because the cost difference between methods is large relative to the loan size:

  • AVM (up to 65% LTV) — £0 cost, minutes to assess, ideal for standard residential in active markets. Best option for small loans where keeping upfront costs down is the priority.
  • Desktop valuation (up to 75% LTV) — low cost, 24–48 hour turnaround. Suitable where the AVM confidence level is insufficient but a physical visit isn't necessary.
  • Full physical inspection (up to 75% LTV) — higher cost and slower, but gives the lender maximum certainty. Used where the property is unusual, rural, or requires the highest possible LTV.

For most small bridging loan enquiries on standard residential property, the AVM route removes the largest upfront cost entirely. We confirm the appropriate route at the point of enquiry — there is nothing to pay before you decide to proceed.
