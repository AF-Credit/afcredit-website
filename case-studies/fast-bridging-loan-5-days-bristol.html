Background

The Chain Break — and Why Speed Was the Only Thing That Mattered

Property chains break constantly in England and Wales. The conventional advice — find a new buyer — takes weeks at minimum and months in slower markets. In this case, the vendor could not afford to wait. They had spent months progressing an onward purchase, had exchanged contracts on it, and were committed to completing on a specific date. Their buyer's withdrawal put everything at risk.

The property was a two-bedroom flat in Redland, one of Bristol's most consistent residential submarkets. Valued at £345,000, the flat had been sale agreed at £342,000. The buyer had funded the purchase with a mortgage — which was declined at the final underwriting stage due to an undisclosed employment change. The withdrawal landed the day before planned exchange.

The vendor's position: they needed to complete their onward purchase within 6 working days or lose the property and their 10% deposit on it. A bridging loan secured against the Redland flat — releasing 60% of its value — would give them the funds to complete. The exit was the subsequent sale of the flat, which they would re-market immediately after moving out.

⚡
How AVM + No-Search Indemnity Cuts 2 Weeks Off a Bridging Loan

Two processes dominate the timeline of a conventional bridging loan: the valuation (typically 5–10 working days for a full RICS physical inspection) and local authority searches (5–20 working days depending on the council). By substituting an instant AVM for the physical survey and a no-search indemnity insurance policy for the actual searches, both waiting periods disappear. At 60% LTV on a standard residential property in a liquid market, the lender's security position is well-protected even without a surveyor visit or search results. In this case, the AVM returned a value of £345,000 within hours of instruction. The no-search indemnity policy was bound the same afternoon. The full legal process, including title review, completion statement and fund transfer, completed in 3 working days alongside credit approval.

Analysis

Why This Worked

  • 60% LTV unlocked the fastest possible process — At 60% of a £345,000 AVM value, the lender held a 40% equity cushion. This justified AVM valuation (no surveyor visit) and no-search indemnity (no search wait). Both shortcuts are standard at this LTV on standard residential.
  • AVM returned the value in hours, not days — Redland is one of the UK's most liquid and well-documented residential submarkets. Abundant comparable data means AVM models are reliable and confident. The £345,000 figure was consistent with the vendor's own estate agent's valuation.
  • No-search indemnity absorbed the searches risk — The indemnity policy was bound the same day at a cost of under £200. It covered all material search categories. For a first-charge lender at 60% LTV in an established residential area, the residual risk was negligible.
  • Clean leasehold title removed legal complexity — 104 years unexpired, no adverse entries, no third-party charges or restrictions. The solicitor was able to clear title review in a single day.
  • Exit was demonstrably achievable — Redland Bristol averages under 60 days on market. The flat eventually sold in 9 weeks at £350,000 — above the original agreed price. The exit modelling was conservative and the outturn was better.

Background

The Chain Break — and Why Speed Was the Only Thing That Mattered

Property chains break constantly in England and Wales. The conventional advice — find a new buyer — takes weeks at minimum and months in slower markets. In this case, the vendor could not afford to wait. They had spent months progressing an onward purchase, had exchanged contracts on it, and were committed to completing on a specific date. Their buyer's withdrawal put everything at risk.

The property was a two-bedroom flat in Redland, one of Bristol's most consistent residential submarkets. Valued at £345,000, the flat had been sale agreed at £342,000. The buyer had funded the purchase with a mortgage — which was declined at the final underwriting stage due to an undisclosed employment change. The withdrawal landed the day before planned exchange.

The vendor's position: they needed to complete their onward purchase within 6 working days or lose the property and their 10% deposit on it. A bridging loan secured against the Redland flat — releasing 60% of its value — would give them the funds to complete. The exit was the subsequent sale of the flat, which they would re-market immediately after moving out.

⚡
How AVM + No-Search Indemnity Cuts 2 Weeks Off a Bridging Loan

Two processes dominate the timeline of a conventional bridging loan: the valuation (typically 5–10 working days for a full RICS physical inspection) and local authority searches (5–20 working days depending on the council). By substituting an instant AVM for the physical survey and a no-search indemnity insurance policy for the actual searches, both waiting periods disappear. At 60% LTV on a standard residential property in a liquid market, the lender's security position is well-protected even without a surveyor visit or search results. In this case, the AVM returned a value of £345,000 within hours of instruction. The no-search indemnity policy was bound the same afternoon. The full legal process, including title review, completion statement and fund transfer, completed in 3 working days alongside credit approval.
