Background

Refurbished East London Residence — Caught Between a Hard Deadline and a Missing Certificate

The borrower was an experienced property investor who had completed a comprehensive refurbishment and permitted development attic conversion on a semi-detached freehold residence in Leyton, East London. The works brought the property's value to £750,000 — a strong uplift on the pre-works purchase price.

The existing bridging facility had been arranged at the point of purchase to fund the acquisition and works. With the refurbishment complete and an identified buyer in place, the plan had been to repay the bridge from sale proceeds. However, the onward buyer made a Lawful Development Certificate a condition of their purchase — and obtaining one takes time.

The original lender refused to grant any extension, leaving the borrower facing the prospect of a forced sale, a breach of the loan agreement, or finding a new lender at pace.

🏗️
Why the Missing LDC Was Not a Blocker

A Lawful Development Certificate confirms that permitted development works comply with planning regulations. It is not the same as building control sign-off — which the borrower already held. AF Credit's underwriting assessed the evidence of completed works directly: building control approval, contractor documentation and a physical inspection of the finished property. Because the works were genuinely complete and the LTV was conservative at 65%, the outstanding LDC was manageable within the loan structure. The 12-month term gave the borrower sufficient time to obtain the certificate and complete the sale.

Analysis

Why This Case Was Possible Without a Valuation

  • Works genuinely complete — Building control approval confirmed the refurbishment and attic conversion had been completed to the required standard. The post-works value was well evidenced.
  • Conservative LTV — At 65% of the post-works value, there was sufficient headroom to underwrite the case without a new physical inspection.
  • Strong comparable evidence — Recent comparable sales in Leyton supported the £750,000 post-works valuation without requiring a surveyor to attend.
  • No-search indemnity — Rather than waiting for local authority search results, an indemnity policy was arranged — removing a further 2–3 weeks from the process.
  • Clear exit route — An identified buyer was already in place. The 12-month term provided time to obtain the LDC and complete the sale without pressure.
  • Experienced borrower — Prior refurbishment track record and a credible, well-structured exit strategy supported the lending decision.
The Outcome

Result

AF Credit completed the £487,500 rebridge in 10 working days, repaying the existing lender before the hard deadline was reached. By underwriting against the full post-works value without requiring a new survey or local authority searches, the timeline was compressed significantly.

The borrower retained the property with 12 months to obtain the Lawful Development Certificate and conclude the sale — without the pressure of an imminently expiring loan.
