Background

When Your Bridge Lender Won't Extend — and Your Exit Isn't Quite Ready

If your bridging lender has refused to extend your loan, you are not alone. Many borrowers find themselves with completed refurbishment works but one outstanding legal or planning document preventing a clean exit. The choice, without a rebridge, is stark: a distressed sale at below-market value, a breach of the loan agreement, or a desperate search for a new lender under time pressure.

In this case, the borrower was an experienced property investor who had completed a comprehensive refurbishment and permitted development attic conversion on a semi-detached freehold residence in Leyton, East London. The finished property had a post-works value of £750,000 — a significant uplift on the pre-works purchase price.

The original bridging facility had been drawn to fund the purchase and works. With refurbishment complete and a buyer identified, the plan was to repay from sale proceeds. However, the buyer required a Lawful Development Certificate before exchange — and with local authority processing times running into weeks or months depending on workload, the existing lender's deadline arrived first.

The existing lender refused any extension. The borrower needed to refinance their bridge quickly — ideally without the delay and cost of commissioning a new physical valuation on a property where works had only just completed.

🏗️
Why No Physical Valuation Was Required

Many refurbishment bridges still require a physical valuation once works complete. In this case, AF Credit did not require one. Because building control had already signed off the refurbishment and attic conversion, comparable sales in Leyton clearly supported the £750,000 post-works value, and the rebridge represented only 65% LTV — a desktop underwriting approach was sufficient. There was no need to instruct a surveyor, wait for an appointment or pay a valuation fee. The borrower had the breathing room they needed, and the no-valuation approach cut a further week from the process.

Analysis

When Can a Refurbishment Bridge Be Completed Without a Valuation?

A physical valuation is not always necessary. Where a property has recently been refurbished, building control has signed off the works, comparable evidence is strong and the loan-to-value is conservative, a lender with the right underwriting capability can often complete a no-valuation rebridge using a desktop assessment instead of a physical inspection. This reduces both cost and completion time — sometimes by a week or more.

In this case, four factors made a valuation-free approach possible:

  • Building control sign-off confirmed works were complete — The refurbishment and attic conversion had been signed off by the local authority building control team. This gave AF Credit confidence in the quality and completeness of the works without needing a surveyor to attend.
  • Strong comparable evidence supported the post-works value — Recent sales of comparable properties in Leyton clearly supported the £750,000 valuation. The desktop underwriting assessment did not require a physical inspection to confirm value.
  • Conservative LTV provided significant headroom — At 65% of the post-works value, there was a 35% equity cushion. The conservative loan-to-value meant the risk profile was manageable without the additional comfort of a new physical report.
  • No-search indemnity removed the final delay — Rather than waiting weeks for local authority search results, an indemnity policy was arranged in hours — protecting against any undisclosed issues while removing the search turnaround time entirely.
  • Clear exit route was already in place — An identified buyer was contracted, subject only to the LDC. The 12-month term provided structured time to obtain the certificate and complete the sale without pressure.
  • Experienced borrower with a track record — Prior refurbishment history and a clearly structured exit supported the lending decision and gave AF Credit confidence in the overall position.
