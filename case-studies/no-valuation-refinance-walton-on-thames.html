The situation

Prime Surrey Residence, Unencumbered — Capital Needed Fast

The borrower was an experienced property developer with substantial UK property holdings. He owned a premium detached family home in one of Surrey's most sought-after gated communities in Walton-on-Thames — unencumbered, in excellent condition, and well below any leverage threshold that would concern a lender.

Crucially, a two-year-old RICS valuation report already existed, prepared for a previous lender and never used. Most lenders would reject it automatically. AF Credit took a different approach.

What no-valuation bridging actually means

No New Survey. Not No Due Diligence.

No-valuation bridging does not mean lending without understanding value. It means obtaining and assessing valuation evidence through alternative means — and applying the same rigour differently.

In practice, the process involves desktop analysis of comparable evidence, title and planning assessment, review of existing reports, and stress-testing conclusions against current market conditions. The output is the same: a well-informed lending decision. The difference is the route — and the time and cost saved along the way.

At low LTV against high-quality residential assets in established prime locations, the evidential bar for a no-valuation decision is lower. At 40% LTV against a well-maintained freehold in Walton-on-Thames, the margin of safety is substantial regardless of modest valuation movement over time.

How the deal was structured

Auditing the Existing Report Rather Than Rejecting It

Rather than instructing a new surveyor, AF Credit audited the existing two-year-old RICS report directly. The methodology was reviewed for soundness. Comparable evidence was assessed for continued relevance against current Walton-on-Thames market conditions. The conclusions were stress-tested. The report was accepted without reassignment.

The borrower incurred no surveyor fees and no valuation delays. The family office funder's requirements were satisfied. AF Credit managed end-to-end coordination with legal teams and achieved completion within the borrower's required timeline.

Most lenders automatically reject valuation reports prepared for previous lenders. AF Credit assessed the merits of the existing report independently — and where those merits stood up, proceeded on that basis. That distinction is what made this transaction possible.

Why this case worked for a no-valuation approach

  • 40% LTV provided substantial security margin — well within no-valuation lending parameters
  • Prime freehold residential asset in an established, liquid Surrey location with deep comparable evidence
  • Existing RICS report was professionally prepared to a high standard and contained robust comparable data
  • Two-year vintage was acceptable given low leverage and stable local market conditions
  • Unencumbered asset with no prior charges simplified title review significantly
  • Experienced borrower with verifiable UK property portfolio and credible exit strategy
  • UHNW private capital funder enabled faster underwriting than institutional channels
The broader point

Speed Is a Lending Decision, Not an Accident

The commercial opportunity this borrower was pursuing had a fixed deadline. There was no room to instruct a surveyor, wait for availability, wait for the report, and then underwrite. The transaction either happened quickly or it did not happen.

No-valuation bridging exists precisely for these situations. Where the asset quality is high, the leverage is conservative, and existing evidence is available, the conventional survey process adds time and cost without adding meaningful risk management. Removing it is not a shortcut — it is the appropriate decision given the facts.
