Can you get a bridging loan without a valuation?

Yes — Although Not Every Property Will Qualify

A no valuation bridging loan does not mean lending without understanding the property's value. Instead, it means using existing valuation evidence, desktop analysis, comparable sales and experienced underwriting to determine whether a new physical inspection is genuinely necessary.

Where appropriate, this approach can reduce completion times by several days while eliminating valuation costs. At AF Credit, every case is assessed individually. Factors such as loan-to-value, property type, location, existing valuation evidence and exit strategy all influence whether a new valuation is required.

Why AF Credit didn't require a new survey

Auditing the Existing Report Rather Than Rejecting It

Rather than instructing another surveyor, AF Credit carried out its own underwriting assessment using the existing RICS report together with current comparable evidence and market analysis. The original valuation methodology was reviewed, recent market movement was considered, and the property's security position was stress-tested.

Because the loan represented only 40% LTV against a prime residential property with substantial equity, commissioning another valuation would have provided little additional benefit while increasing both costs and timescales.

Unlike many lenders who insist on a new valuation regardless of the circumstances, AF Credit considers whether existing evidence remains sufficient before deciding whether another inspection is necessary. This approach allowed the borrower to move quickly without compromising underwriting standards.

Why this property qualified for a no valuation bridging loan

  • The loan represented a conservative 40% loan-to-value
  • High-quality freehold residence in a well-established Surrey location with strong comparable evidence
  • A professionally prepared RICS valuation already existed
  • Current comparable sales supported the existing valuation conclusions
  • The borrower owned the property outright with no existing charges
  • Experienced borrower with extensive property portfolio and a credible refinance exit

The broader point

A Faster Lending Decision

The borrower had a fixed deadline to complete a commercial transaction. Waiting for surveyor availability, arranging an inspection and receiving a formal report would have delayed completion without materially improving the lending decision.

By reviewing existing valuation evidence alongside current market data, AF Credit was able to complete the refinance efficiently while maintaining prudent underwriting standards. If you are refinancing a property with significant equity and an existing valuation report, there is a good chance a similar approach could be considered.
