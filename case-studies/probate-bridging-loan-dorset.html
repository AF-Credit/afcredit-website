The Challenge

The estate's principal asset was a detached house in Dorset valued at £875,000, but much of the estate's wealth remained tied up in the property.

Although probate had already been granted, the executor needed access to funds before the property sale completed in order to continue administering the estate and meet outstanding financial obligations.

Rather than rushing the property onto the market or accepting a lower offer to release funds quickly, the executor wanted the flexibility to market the property properly while accessing capital immediately.

They required a lender capable of completing quickly and lending against the inherited property without unnecessary delays.

How AF Credit Helped

AF Credit assessed the inherited property, the executor's legal authority and the proposed exit strategy before approving a first charge probate bridging loan.

Because the borrowing represented only 32% of the property's value, a desktop valuation was sufficient, avoiding the need for a physical inspection and helping reduce both costs and completion times.

The facility comprised:

  • Gross loan£299,687.19
  • Net advance£280,000
  • Term6 months
  • Monthly interest rate0.95%, fully retained
  • ValuationDesktop
  • ChargeFirst legal charge

With the interest retained for the duration of the loan, the executor had no monthly interest payments to manage while the property was being marketed.

From the initial enquiry to completion, we released funds in just 12 days.

The Outcome

The loan gave the executor immediate access to £280,000 without needing to sell the inherited property under time pressure.

The funding allowed the estate to:

  • continue the administration process without delay;
  • settle outstanding estate liabilities;
  • retain flexibility over when to sell the property;
  • maximise the eventual sale price rather than accepting a quick sale; and
  • repay the bridging loan in full from the sale proceeds.

The property was subsequently sold for £862,000, and the loan was redeemed in full.

Why a Probate Bridging Loan Was the Right Solution

Probate bridging finance is designed for situations where substantial wealth is tied up in inherited property but immediate access to capital is required.

Executors often need funding before a property sale completes for reasons including:

  • settling estate liabilities;
  • funding estate administration costs;
  • making interim distributions to beneficiaries;
  • carrying out improvements before marketing; or
  • avoiding the pressure of an immediate sale.

A probate bridging loan provides short-term funding while allowing the property to be sold at the right time rather than simply as quickly as possible.
