The Opportunity

The property was a three-bedroom Victorian terrace in Nottingham that hadn't been modernised for decades. Although structurally sound, it required a new kitchen, a new bathroom, complete redecoration, replacement windows and a full electrical rewire.

Because of its condition, mainstream mortgage lenders would have been unwilling to lend without the repairs being completed first. At the same time, the seller wanted a buyer who could complete quickly — making speed just as important as funding.

Recognising the potential to add value, the developer secured the property for £205,000 — well below the value of comparable refurbished homes in the same area, which were selling in the £280,000–£300,000 range.

How AF Credit Helped

AF Credit assessed the application and agreed terms directly, without the need for committee approval or third-party referral. Following a valuation confirming the property's current market value at £205,000, funding was agreed at 75% LTV — enabling completion within two weeks of the initial enquiry.

Because the refurbishment involved cosmetic improvements rather than structural alterations, the loan was advanced in a single drawdown at completion. There were no staged payments and no monitoring surveyor visits — allowing the developer to start work immediately.

The Result

The purchase completed in 14 days. Over the following eleven weeks the developer completed a new fitted kitchen, a modern bathroom, full electrical rewiring, replacement windows and redecoration throughout.

Once finished, the property was marketed for sale and attracted strong interest. It ultimately sold for £289,500 — allowing the bridging loan to be repaid in full and generating a successful return on the project.

Why a Bridging Loan Was the Right Choice

Traditional mortgages often struggle with properties requiring significant refurbishment. Even where finance is available, lengthy application times can cause buyers to lose attractive opportunities. In this case, a refurbishment bridging loan allowed the developer to:

  • complete within the seller's required timescale;
  • secure a property well below market value;
  • start renovation immediately after completion;
  • add significant value through refurbishment; and
  • repay the loan from the sale proceeds.

Without bridging finance, the opportunity would almost certainly have been lost.
