The Challenge

The borrower had found an attractive investment opportunity in Beeston, Nottingham.

Although the property was structurally sound, it required significant modernisation before it could achieve its full market value. The kitchen, bathroom, electrics and windows all needed replacing, together with complete internal redecoration.

The seller wanted certainty and speed.

Because the property required refurbishment, obtaining a traditional residential mortgage within the required timescale was unlikely. The borrower needed funding that could complete quickly without compromising the purchase.

How AF Credit Helped

After reviewing the property and the proposed refurbishment project, our underwriting team approved a refurbishment bridging loan secured by a first legal charge over the property.

A full RICS valuation confirmed the property's current market value at £205,000, together with an estimated post-refurbishment value of approximately £295,000 based on comparable sales in the area.

As the proposed works were entirely cosmetic, we structured the loan using a single drawdown, allowing the borrower to receive all funds on completion without the delays and additional costs associated with staged releases and monitoring surveyors.

The borrower funded the refurbishment works separately using their own capital, enabling the project to commence immediately after we released funds.

The Outcome

We released funds and completed the purchase in 14 days from the initial enquiry.

Within 48 hours of completion, contractors were on site carrying out the refurbishment programme.

Over the following eleven weeks the property underwent:

  • Installation of a new kitchen
  • Installation of a new bathroom
  • Complete electrical rewire
  • Replacement windows
  • Full internal decoration

Once the works were completed, the property was marketed for sale. A buyer was secured within three weeks, with the property selling for £289,500. The bridging loan was repaid in full from the sale proceeds, allowing the borrower to successfully complete the project and realise the uplift in value created through the refurbishment.

Why a Refurbishment Bridging Loan Was the Right Solution

Properties requiring refurbishment often present excellent investment opportunities, but they can also create challenges when obtaining traditional mortgage finance.

Many lenders are unwilling to lend on properties that require substantial improvement or are unlikely to complete within a seller's required timescale.

In this case, a refurbishment bridging loan from AF Credit allowed the borrower to:

  • Complete the purchase within the seller's deadline.
  • Secure a property below market value.
  • Begin refurbishment immediately after completion.
  • Add significant value through cosmetic improvements.
  • Repay the loan following the successful sale of the completed property.

Without short-term finance, the opportunity would likely have been lost.

FAQs

Can You Get a Bridging Loan for a Property That Needs Refurbishment?

Yes.

AF Credit regularly provides refurbishment bridging loans for borrowers purchasing properties that require improvement before they can be sold, refinanced or let.

We can consider funding for projects involving:

  • Cosmetic refurbishments
  • Buy-to-let renovations
  • Auction purchases
  • Below-market-value opportunities
  • Properties unsuitable for conventional mortgages
  • Residential investment projects

Every application is assessed individually, taking into account the property, the borrower's experience and the proposed exit strategy.

We lend up to 75% LTV on light refurbishment projects. The LTV is assessed against the current market value of the property in its present condition — not the projected post-refurbishment GDV.

Where refurbishment works are cosmetic rather than structural, we release the full loan in a single drawdown at completion. There are no staged payments, no monitoring surveyor visits and no additional drawdown costs. The borrower funds the works from their own capital and repays the bridge from the eventual sale or refinance proceeds.

Light refurbishment covers cosmetic works — new kitchen, bathroom, decoration, flooring, replacement windows, rewiring — with no structural changes. Heavy refurbishment involves structural alterations, extensions, loft conversions or change of use. Our standard refurbishment bridging loan is structured as a single drawdown for light cosmetic works. Heavy refurbishment with staged drawdowns requires a different structure — contact us to discuss your project.
