What is it?

What is a desktop valuation bridging loan?

A desktop valuation bridging loan is a short-term property loan where we accept a RICS-qualified remote valuation instead of a physical on-site inspection. Our underwriting team uses that valuation as the basis for our lending decision — and because no surveyor needs to travel to and inspect the property, the process is faster and cheaper than a full RICS inspection.

Surveyor-led, not algorithmic

A qualified RICS valuer assesses the property remotely using comparable sales, Land Registry data and local market knowledge. This is a professional opinion — not a computer model — which means our underwriting team can rely on it for up to 75% LTV lending.

No site visit required

The valuer does not need to physically visit the property. That eliminates the scheduling delay that often adds days to a physical inspection — and removes the surveyor travel fee from the bill. The result is delivered in 48–72 hours.

Same LTV as a full inspection

AF Credit will lend up to 75% LTV on a desktop valuation. Most bridging lenders cap desktop valuations below their physical inspection LTV. We do not — a qualified remote opinion gives us the same confidence on eligible properties.

Why does this matter? For borrowers, it means you can access the same leverage as a full inspection — without paying for a site visit or waiting for surveyor availability. For time-critical deals, that can be the difference between making a deadline and missing it.

The key differentiator

75% LTV without a surveyor visit

This is the headline. Most bridging lenders that offer desktop valuations cap them at 65%–70% LTV. AF Credit lends up to 75% — the same ceiling as a full physical RICS inspection. Here is why that matters in practice.

More borrowing power on the same property

On a £400,000 property, the difference between 70% and 75% LTV is £20,000 of additional net loan. For a borrower using a bridge to fund an acquisition or release equity, that gap is material.

On the same property, 75% LTV = £300,000 gross loan. At a competitor capping desktop at 70%, the gross loan is £280,000. The cost of the shortfall is not just £20,000 — it is whatever you would otherwise need to fund it from (savings, a second charge, reduced purchase confidence).

Lower cost than a full inspection

A full RICS physical inspection typically costs more than a desktop valuation — because a surveyor must travel to and inspect the property in person. That difference is not trivial, particularly on smaller loans.

With AF Credit's desktop valuation route, you get 75% LTV leverage at a lower valuation cost. You do not need to pay for a physical inspection to access our maximum LTV.

Valuation routes

Desktop vs AVM vs full RICS inspection

AF Credit offers three valuation routes. Each suits different property types, timelines and loan sizes. Here is how they compare.

Valuation route How it works Max LTV Turnaround Best for
AVM (Automated) Computer algorithm using Hometrack data. No human involvement. 65% LTV Instant (minutes) Standard residential in active markets with reliable data. Fastest and free — but narrowest coverage.
Desktop valuation This page RICS-qualified valuer assesses remotely. Comparables, Land Registry, market evidence. 75% LTV 48–72 hours Standard to mid-complexity residential. Wider property coverage than AVM, same LTV as full inspection.
Full RICS inspection Surveyor physically inspects the property. Most comprehensive report. 75% LTV 5–10 working days Complex, unusual, rural or high-value properties. Heaviest refurbishment cases.

The takeaway: Desktop valuation sits in the sweet spot. It covers a wider range of properties than an AVM and delivers 75% LTV — the same maximum as a full physical inspection — but returns in 48–72 hours rather than 5–10 working days, and at lower cost. For most standard residential bridging cases where speed matters, desktop is the right choice.

Property criteria

Which properties are suitable?

Desktop valuations work best where a RICS valuer can form a confident opinion from available market evidence. The stronger the comparable data in the area, the more reliable the remote assessment.

Well suited to desktop valuation

  • Standard residential houses and flats
  • Buy-to-let investment properties
  • Conventional construction (brick, tile, cavity wall)
  • Properties in areas with active sales and good comparable evidence
  • Properties with light cosmetic refurbishment planned
  • Auction purchases where speed is essential

May require full RICS inspection

  • Unusual, high-value or unique assets
  • Rural locations with limited comparable evidence
  • Listed buildings or heavily modified structures
  • Non-standard construction (timber frame, steel frame, prefab)
  • Properties requiring heavy structural refurbishment or development
  • Complex mixed-use or commercial properties

If you are unsure whether your property is suitable for a desktop valuation, call us before paying any fees. We will tell you which route is viable upfront — including whether our AVM route works, which is faster still on eligible properties.

Use cases

When desktop valuation bridging is the right choice

Desktop valuation bridging solves a specific problem: you need the leverage of a full RICS inspection but cannot wait 5–10 working days for a physical survey. These are the scenarios where we most commonly use this route.

Auction purchases

Auction contracts require completion in 28 days — sometimes less. A physical survey cannot always be arranged and completed within that window. Our desktop route can be instructed and returned within 48–72 hours, keeping the deal on track. We provide same-day indicative terms on auction enquiries.

Chain breaks and time-critical purchases

When a vendor needs certainty and your buyer has fallen through, a bridge funded by a desktop valuation can complete in days rather than weeks. No need to wait for surveyor availability. We release funds once legal due diligence is complete — often within 5–10 working days of formal application.

Refinance and re-bridge

If you are approaching the end of an existing bridging term, a desktop valuation re-bridge can be arranged quickly to protect you from penalty interest. A RICS remote assessment gives our underwriting team the valuation basis to move at speed — without requiring a full physical inspection on a property you may have recently purchased.

Light refurbishment

A desktop valuation can support lending where cosmetic works are planned — new kitchen, bathroom, redecoration. A qualified valuer can assess the property's current condition and the impact of planned light works from comparable evidence. For heavier or structural refurbishment, we would commission a full inspection.

Buy before you sell

You have found the property you want but have not yet sold your existing home. A desktop-valued bridge gives you the funds to proceed immediately — at up to 75% LTV — while your sale progresses. When your property sells, you repay the bridge.

Equity release from an owned property

If you own a property unencumbered or with low existing debt, a desktop-valued bridge can release equity quickly without requiring a physical inspection. We assess the property remotely and can release funds within days of credit approval.

Step 4
Completion — typically 5–10 working days
Once underwriting is satisfied and solicitors have completed their due diligence, we release funds. On clean cases with a responsive solicitor, completion lands within 5–10 working days of formal application.

How to keep your timeline tight

  • Provide the property address and any existing mortgage details upfront
  • Have photo ID and proof of address ready when you enquire
  • Instruct a responsive, experienced solicitor on day one — legal delays are the most common cause of timeline slippage
  • Be specific about your exit strategy — sale evidence, refinance plan, or a credible alternative
  • For properties with works, provide a clear scope and budget at enquiry

FAQs

Desktop valuation bridging — questions answered

The questions our team gets asked on almost every desktop valuation enquiry.

What is a desktop valuation bridging loan?

A desktop valuation bridging loan is a short-term property loan where AF Credit, as direct lender, accepts a RICS-qualified remote valuation instead of a physical site inspection. A qualified valuer assesses the property using comparable sales, Land Registry data and local market evidence — without visiting the property. This reduces cost and time versus a full RICS inspection while still giving our underwriting team a proper valuation basis to lend against.

What LTV can I get with a desktop valuation?

AF Credit lends up to 75% LTV on desktop-valued properties — the same maximum as a full physical RICS inspection. This is a genuine differentiator: most bridging lenders cap desktop valuations at 65%–70% LTV, below their inspection-based ceiling. The exact LTV on your deal depends on the property type, location, exit strength and your profile.

How does desktop valuation differ from an AVM?

An AVM (Automated Valuation Model) is a computer algorithm — fast and free, but limited in coverage and capped at 65% LTV. A desktop valuation is produced by a RICS-qualified human valuer who can interpret property specification, light works context and nuances that an algorithm cannot. Desktop valuations cover a wider range of properties, support up to 75% LTV, and can factor in circumstances that the AVM model would miss.

How fast can a desktop valuation bridging loan complete?

AF Credit typically completes desktop valuation bridging loans in 5–10 working days from formal application. The desktop valuation itself takes 48–72 hours. By instructing the valuation and solicitors on the same day — running both in parallel — we avoid the delays that build up when processes are run sequentially.

Is a desktop valuation cheaper than a full RICS inspection?

Yes. A desktop valuation typically costs less than a full physical inspection because no surveyor site visit is required — there is no travel, no appointment, no access to arrange. For a borrower, the saving is meaningful, particularly on smaller loan sizes where valuation fees are a significant proportion of total transaction costs.

What property types are suitable for a desktop valuation?

Desktop valuations work best on standard residential houses, flats and buy-to-let properties with conventional construction and good comparable evidence in the area. Properties that may require a full inspection include unusual or high-value assets, rural properties with limited comparables, listed buildings, heavily modified structures and non-standard construction. Call us upfront — we will tell you which route is viable before you commit any fees.

Can I use a desktop valuation bridging loan for an auction purchase?

Yes. Desktop valuation bridging is well suited to auction purchases where the 28-day completion deadline makes a physical inspection difficult to arrange. We can issue same-day indicative terms on auction enquiries and instruct the desktop valuation immediately on acceptance of our terms, keeping your timeline tight.

Is light refurbishment allowed with a desktop valuation?

Yes. We can lend against properties where light cosmetic refurbishment is planned — new kitchen, bathroom, flooring, redecoration. A RICS-qualified desktop valuer can assess the property's current condition and the impact of planned cosmetic works from comparable evidence. For heavy structural refurbishment or development, we commission a full inspection-based valuation.

Do I need to prove income?

Not typically. AF Credit's bridging underwriting is primarily asset-led — we focus on the property, the LTV and the exit strategy. Income evidence may be requested for regulated cases where you or a close family member will occupy the property, but the majority of desktop valuation bridging is for investment or commercial purposes and does not require income proof.

What exit strategies do you accept?

The two most common exits are sale of the property and refinance onto a longer-term mortgage. We assess your proposed exit at enquiry — we want to understand clearly how you will repay before we lend. A credible, evidenced exit is central to every case we fund.

What is the minimum and maximum loan size?

AF Credit lends from £26,000 to £2,000,000 on desktop valuation bridging loans across England and Wales.

Are there early repayment charges?

No early repayment charge after the minimum 3-month term. If your exit completes ahead of the loan term, you repay the outstanding balance plus accrued interest to the redemption date — nothing more.
