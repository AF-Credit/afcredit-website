Qualification

Can my property use a desktop valuation?

A desktop valuation works where a RICS-qualified valuer can form a confident opinion from available market evidence without inspecting the property in person. These are the conditions that make it viable.

A desktop valuation is more likely to work where:

  • The property is standard residential or buy-to-let
  • The property is in reasonable habitable condition
  • There are recent comparable sales in the area
  • The construction is conventional (brick, tile, cavity wall)
  • The property is not highly unusual or one-of-a-kind
  • The LTV is proportionate to the risk and exit
  • The exit is clear — sale, refinance or another evidenced route

We confirm the likely valuation route before you pay any valuation fee. Tell us the property address and we will confirm desktop, AVM or full inspection — before you commit any costs.

When a desktop valuation may not be possible: If the property is rural with few comparables, is listed, non-standard construction, or its condition significantly affects its value, a physical inspection may be required. We will tell you this upfront. There is no fee to find out.

What if you're not sure?

Call us or send the property address. We will confirm within hours whether a desktop valuation is viable, what LTV is realistic, and whether there are any property-specific concerns before you proceed. This costs nothing and commits you to nothing.

What is it?

What is a desktop valuation bridging loan?

A desktop valuation bridging loan is short-term property finance where the lender accepts a professional remote valuation instead of a physical inspection. The valuation is still real — it is produced by a RICS-qualified valuer using comparable sales, Land Registry records and local market data — but the valuer does not visit the property.

A real valuation, done remotely

A desktop valuation is not an estimate or an algorithm. A qualified human valuer reviews the evidence and issues a professional opinion. Our underwriting team uses that opinion as the basis for the lending decision — which is why we can lend up to 75% LTV on the result.

No site visit, no scheduling delay

Removing the physical visit eliminates one of the main causes of delay in bridging — waiting for a surveyor to travel to and inspect the property. No access needs to be arranged. The valuation is returned typically within 48–72 hours of instruction.

Direct lender decision

AF Credit is a direct principal lender. We make our own credit decisions — no broker, no external credit committee. When the desktop valuation is returned, our underwriting team reviews it directly and moves into legals. That means fewer steps and faster responses.

Important distinction: A desktop valuation bridging loan is not the same as a "no valuation" bridging loan. There is a valuation — it is just conducted remotely by a qualified professional rather than in person. If you are looking for a route with no valuation at all, see our no valuation bridging loans page which covers AVM and existing report routes.

Honesty first

When a desktop valuation will not work

We would rather tell you this upfront than after you have paid fees. A desktop valuation has limitations, and some properties or scenarios will require a full physical inspection before we can lend.

Heavy refurbishment or development

Desktop valuations can support light cosmetic works where a valuer can assess condition and impact from available evidence. Where structural work, conversion, extension or development is planned, a physical inspection is needed to assess current condition, scope of works and GDV.

Unusual or complex property

Highly individual assets — unusual architecture, very large footprints, unique location or mixed-use exposure — often have limited or no comparable evidence. A desktop valuer cannot form a reliable opinion without it. A physical inspection gives our team the detail needed to make a credit decision.

Non-standard construction

Timber frame, steel frame, prefabricated, concrete panel, single-skin brick and similar construction types carry risks that a remote assessment cannot fully evaluate. Condition and structural integrity can be critical to value in a way that a desktop process cannot address.

Rural or low-transaction markets

A desktop valuation relies on comparable sales data. Where transactions are infrequent — rural areas, isolated locations, specialist rural property — a valuer may not be able to support a confident opinion remotely.

Listed buildings

Listed status affects what can be done to a property and can significantly influence value, maintenance obligations and exit route. A physical inspection allows a valuer to assess the property's actual condition and listing implications in a way that remote review typically cannot.

Commercial-only property

AF Credit's desktop valuation route is for residential and buy-to-let property. Pure commercial assets — offices, retail units, industrial premises — require a different valuation approach. See our commercial bridging page for more.

If your property falls into any of these categories, we will tell you at enquiry stage — before any valuation fee is instructed. We may still be able to lend, but with a full physical inspection rather than desktop. The enquiry is free and same-day.

Important distinction

Desktop valuation is not the same as no valuation

This is a distinction worth being clear about, because the two terms are often used interchangeably in the market when they describe different things.

Desktop valuation bridging loan

A professional valuation is still produced. A RICS-qualified valuer reviews the property remotely using comparable evidence and issues an opinion of value. There is a valuation fee. The valuation report is a real professional document. Because we have a qualified opinion to underwrite against, we can lend up to 75% LTV on eligible properties.

The difference from a full inspection is the method — remote rather than physical — not whether a valuation takes place.

No valuation bridging loan

AF Credit's no-valuation route uses either an AVM (automated algorithm output) or an existing RICS valuation report, with no new professional assessment commissioned. There is no desktop valuation fee in this route. AVM is capped at 65% LTV and is limited to standard residential property that meets the confidence threshold of the automated model.

See our no valuation bridging page and AVM bridging page for more detail on those routes.

Where desktop fits: Desktop valuation sits between AVM and full physical inspection. It provides a human professional opinion at lower cost and faster turnaround than a full inspection, covering a wider property range than AVM. It is the right route where AVM cannot support the case but a full inspection would be excessive or too slow.

  • For auction purchases, provide the lot details and auction pack immediately on agreement of terms

FAQs

Desktop valuation bridging — questions answered

Straightforward answers to the questions we get asked on most desktop valuation enquiries.

Is a desktop valuation accepted by bridging lenders?

Yes. Desktop valuations are widely used across the bridging market. The level of acceptance varies by lender, property type and case risk profile. AF Credit, as a direct lender, confirms upfront whether a desktop valuation is viable for a specific property — before you pay any valuation fee.

Is a desktop valuation the same as an AVM?

No. An AVM (Automated Valuation Model) is a computer algorithm — fast, free, but narrow in coverage and typically capped at 65% LTV. A desktop valuation is produced by a RICS-qualified human valuer using comparable sales, Land Registry data and local market knowledge. Desktop valuations cover a wider range of properties, can factor in light refurbishment context, and support up to 75% LTV with AF Credit. They are different products.

Can I get 75% LTV with a desktop valuation?

Yes, with AF Credit. We lend up to 75% LTV on desktop-valued properties — the same maximum as a full physical RICS inspection. Most bridging lenders cap desktop valuations at 65%–70% LTV. The actual LTV on your specific case depends on the property type, location, exit strength and your profile, and is subject to underwriting and the valuation outcome.

Does the surveyor visit the property?

No. A desktop valuation is completed remotely — the valuer does not need to physically inspect the property. No access needs to be arranged. The valuer uses comparable sales data, Land Registry records and local market evidence to form a professional opinion. The result is typically returned within 48–72 hours of instruction.

Can I use a desktop valuation for auction finance?

Yes. Desktop valuation bridging is well suited to auction purchases where the 28-day completion deadline makes a physical inspection difficult to arrange. We issue same-day indicative terms on auction enquiries and can instruct the desktop valuation immediately on agreement of terms, keeping your timeline tight.

Is a desktop valuation suitable for refurbishment bridging?

For light cosmetic refurbishment — new kitchen, bathroom, flooring, decoration — yes. A RICS-qualified desktop valuer can assess current condition and factor in the impact of planned works from comparable evidence. For heavy structural refurbishment, conversion or development, a full physical inspection is typically required, as condition and build scope are critical to the valuation.

What happens if the desktop valuation comes in lower than expected?

If the desktop valuation is below the anticipated value, the available loan is calculated against the valuation figure, not the purchase price or your estimate. This can reduce the LTV and net advance. AF Credit always informs you of the final loan position once the valuation is received. You are not obligated to proceed if the terms change materially from the indicative offer. We try to identify potential valuation concerns at enquiry stage to reduce surprises.

Can I use a desktop valuation for a buy-to-let bridging loan?

Yes. Buy-to-let properties are well suited to desktop valuations where the property is standard residential stock with good comparable evidence in the area. AF Credit considers BTL bridging on desktop-valued properties up to 75% LTV, subject to property type, location and exit strength.

How quickly can funds be released?

AF Credit typically completes desktop valuation bridging loans in 5–10 working days from formal application. The desktop valuation itself takes 48–72 hours. By instructing valuation and solicitors on the same day and running both in parallel, we avoid the delays that stack up in a sequential process. The single biggest variable outside our control is solicitor speed — instruct an experienced solicitor early.

Is this available for regulated bridging loans?

Regulated bridging loans — where you or a close family member intend to occupy the property — are not offered directly by AF Credit. Where a case appears to be regulated, AF Credit may refer the enquiry to an appropriately regulated intermediary. The majority of desktop valuation bridging we fund is unregulated: auction purchases, investment property, BTL, re-bridges and equity release from investment assets.

What is the difference between a desktop valuation and a no-valuation bridging loan?

A desktop valuation bridging loan still involves a real professional valuation — conducted remotely by a RICS-qualified valuer. A no-valuation route uses either an AVM (automated algorithm) or an existing RICS report, with no new valuation commissioned. Desktop valuation sits between AVM and a full physical inspection in terms of cost, speed and property coverage. See our no valuation bridging page for more on those routes.

What types of property are suitable for a desktop valuation?

Desktop valuations work best on standard residential houses, flats and BTL properties with conventional construction and good comparable evidence nearby. Properties that typically require a full physical inspection include rural assets with limited comparables, listed buildings, heavily modified structures, non-standard construction and properties where physical condition materially affects value. We confirm viability at enquiry stage — at no cost to you.

What costs should I expect?

Typical costs include: interest from 0.79% per month (case dependent); an arrangement fee (usually a percentage of the gross loan); a desktop valuation fee (typically lower than a full RICS inspection); and legal fees. Interest can usually be retained, rolled or serviced. No early repayment charge after the minimum 3-month term. All figures are indicative and subject to final underwriting terms.
