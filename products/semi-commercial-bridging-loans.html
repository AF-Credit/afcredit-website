What is semi-commercial?

Mixed-use property — where residential meets commercial.

A semi-commercial property — sometimes called a mixed-use property — is one that combines residential and commercial elements under a single freehold title. The most familiar example is a ground-floor shop, office or takeaway with one or more residential flats on the floors above.

These properties are an extremely common feature of UK high streets and town centres, yet they sit awkwardly in the conventional mortgage market. Residential lenders require a property to be wholly residential — a commercial element, however small, typically disqualifies it. Commercial mortgage lenders may consider mixed-use assets, but their criteria can be restrictive and smaller assets often fall below their minimum loan thresholds.

Bridging finance fills this gap. A semi-commercial bridging loan from AF Credit is secured against the blended value of both elements, assessed by a qualified RICS surveyor, and can complete in 2–4 weeks. We are a direct principal lender with genuine mixed-use appetite — not a lender that tolerates these assets reluctantly.

Underwriting

Why semi-commercial bridging is assessed differently

Semi-commercial property is not simply a residential property with a shop attached, nor is it a pure commercial asset — and any lender that treats it as either is likely to get the pricing wrong. The underwriting approach for semi-commercial bridging has three distinct characteristics that set it apart.

📊

Blended valuation

A RICS surveyor values the residential and commercial elements separately, using comparable evidence appropriate to each. The two figures are then combined to produce the open market value on which the loan is based.

📉

Lower LTV than residential

We lend up to 70% LTV on semi-commercial, compared to 75% on pure residential. The slightly lower LTV reflects the more limited pool of specialist semi-commercial mortgage lenders at exit.

🎯

Fewer lenders operate here

Far fewer lenders have the appetite, expertise and systems to underwrite semi-commercial efficiently. AF Credit is one of a small number of direct principal lenders that genuinely understands this asset class.

Valuation

How semi-commercial property is valued — step by step

Semi-commercial valuation is more involved than a standard residential or commercial assessment — because the property is both. Here is exactly how it works.

1

RICS mixed-use surveyor appointed

AF Credit instructs a RICS-qualified surveyor from our approved panel with specific mixed-use experience. Not all surveyors are equipped to value semi-commercial property — the surveyor must be comfortable in both the residential comparables market and the commercial yield-based methodology.

2

Physical inspection of both elements

The surveyor attends and inspects the full property — both the commercial ground floor and the residential upper floors. Condition, layout, tenancy status, and any permitted development or planning history are all assessed in person. Desktop valuation is not suitable for this asset class.

3

Residential element valued on comparables

The residential flats or accommodation are valued using comparable residential sales — the same methodology used on a standard residential valuation. Lease length, floor area, condition, and local comparable evidence all feed into this figure.

4

Commercial element valued on yield or comparables

The commercial unit is valued on either a yield basis (if tenanted — passing rent capitalised at an appropriate yield) or on comparable evidence for vacant commercial units in the area. The surveyor assesses covenant strength, lease terms, and any break clauses where relevant.

5

Blended open market value produced

The two valuations are combined into a single blended open market value — the figure on which AF Credit calculates the loan and the LTV. The report is reviewed by our credit team and, where it supports the loan, a formal offer is issued promptly.
